SHRIMP

HABITAT

Most shrimp live on the seafloor of the ocean, and in lakes and rivers. Nearly all species of shrimp are aquatic, with the exception of the Merguia rhizophorae

DIET

Shrimp eat nearly anything, and since they're omnivores, they'll eat plants and animals. Some things they eat include algae, dead and living plants, and snails

APPEARANCE

They're classified as crustaceans, and are mostly divided into three sections, the head, thorax, and abdomen. They have a tail, ten pairs of legs, eyes, and a rostrum, but body structure varies in different species of shrimp

Extra Info

Pistol shrimp are able to produce a bubble that can stun their prey. They have a special "snapping" claw that can regenerate if cut off. The bubble can reach temperatures over 5000 Kelvin

The Merguia Rhizophorae is a species of shrimp that is semi terrestrial. It was first discovered in 1969, and is the first and only semi terrestrial or terrestrial shrimp as of now

Shrimp is the most popular seafood, being very nutritious, and they're consumed worldwide. They're served in restraunts fried, boiled, in soup, and in dumplings
